The economics

AI use falls to zero as a task repeats.

The first run pays for the intelligence — the LLM writes the recipe. Every run after replays it deterministically, for free. When a page changes, one heal re-teaches the step; auto-capture makes the next heal cheaper, and it settles back to zero.

loop — zsh
# teach it once, then replay like a bot — no LLM, ~$0 per run
$ loop run post-to-linkedin text="We just shipped 🚀"
  · open linkedin.com/feed · compose · click "Post"  → ✓ posted

$ loop run pull-report
  · open the dashboard · extract "table tr" → 42 rows → dishes/report.csv
The story

How Tom built a kitchen that runs itself

An entrepreneur, his recipes, and a brigade of cooks who never sleep.

Chapter one · The dream

Once, an entrepreneur named Tom dreamed of opening a restaurant — and serving dishes the world had never tasted.

You — with a business to grow, and work only you know how to do.

Chapter two · The kitchen

So he built a kitchen and brought in a head chef who could cook anything he was asked.

The kitchen is Loop Browser; the head chef is the CLI you command — with an LLM for a brain.

Chapter three · The recipes

Tom wrote down his secret recipes — and kept his ingredients in the pantry, adding them fresh each cook.

Recipes are your saved commands; ingredients are your data — and both stay yours.

Chapter four · The cooking

Once the head chef perfected a dish, the kitchen plated it flawlessly — again and again — while Tom watched every plate leave the pass.

Automation you can watch — deterministic, repeatable, no guesswork.

Chapter five · The success

Word spread. The restaurant thrived. Today a hundred line cooks work the kitchen — and Tom simply runs the place.

Chef bots replay your recipes at scale. You own the menu; the kitchen does the work.

You hold the key

Your real accounts, automated.

LinkedIn, WhatsApp, your CRM — the sites behind a login that headless cloud agents can't reach. You log in once, in a window you own; it works inside your session, and you watch every step.

Nothing private ever leaves your machine — not your logins, your data, your results, or your private recipes. Only a recipe's method is shareable, and only when you choose. The cook and chef never see your credentials.

Get started

Your first dish, in five steps.

What each piece is, and where to get it.

1

Get Loop

The kitchen — the glass browser you'll command. git clone it from GitHub and run the quick setup.

How to install →
2

Add a brain

The chef's brain — an LLM that writes & heals your recipes. Bring your own.

3

The loop CLI

The command line you cook from — loop run, loop author. On your PATH after setup (or run node cli.mjs from the repo).

4

Connect

Launch Loop — it opens and listens on a local port. Every loop command (yours or the AI's) connects to it instantly. No config, no cloud.

5

Cook

Run a dish — loop run hn-top — or have the chef write one with loop author. Watch every step.
