The story

How Tom became successful

A little tale about an entrepreneur, his recipes, and the cooks who never sleep.

Chapter one · The dream

Once, an entrepreneur named Tom dreamed of opening a restaurant — and serving dishes the world had never tasted.

You — with a business to grow, and work only you know how to do.

Chapter two · The kitchen

So he built a kitchen and brought in a head chef who could cook anything he was asked.

The kitchen is Loop Browser; the head chef is the CLI you command — with an LLM (Claude Code) for a brain.

Chapter three · The recipes

Tom wrote down his secret recipes — and kept his ingredients in the pantry, adding them fresh each cook.

Recipes are your saved commands; ingredients are your data — and both stay yours.

Chapter four · The cooking

Once the head chef perfected a dish, the kitchen plated it flawlessly — again and again — while Tom watched every plate leave the pass.

Automation you can watch — deterministic, repeatable, no guesswork.

Chapter five · The success

Word spread. The restaurant thrived. Today a hundred line cooks work the kitchen — and Tom simply runs the place.

Cheap bots replay your recipes at scale. You own the menu; the kitchen does the work.

The chef's brain — the LLM that writes & heals recipes, on your own subscription.

claude.com/claude-code →
3

The loop CLI

The command line you cook from — loop run, loop author. Ships with the app.

4

Install Node.js

Runs the CLI under the hood. Version 18 or newer — a one-time install.

nodejs.org →
5

Connect

Launch Loop — it opens and listens on a local port. Every loop command (yours or Claude's) connects to it instantly. No config, no cloud.

6

Cook

Run a dish — loop run hn-top — or have the chef write one with loop author. Watch every step.
